What Caused Ariane 5 to Explode

Numbers in computers can be stored in several categories.

The two categories involved in the Ariane 5 explosion are 64-bit floating point numbers, and 16-bit integers.

1. Ariane 5 held horizontal acceleration in a 64-bit floating point number (range of -10308 to 10308).

2. The 64-bit floating point number was converted into a 16-bit signed integer (range of -32,768 to 32,767)

3. When horizontal acceleration reached a number above 32,767, the program was not able to store the number correctly. This is called a integer overflow, and is what caused the explosion[5].
