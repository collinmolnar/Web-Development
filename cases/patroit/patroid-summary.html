Patriot Missile

The Patriot missile is a very important asset to the United States Army, and armies all over the world. It is a surface-to-air missile used to intercept aircraft, and also other ballistic missiles[11]. It was used heavily during the Persian Gulf war against Iraqi missiles that were fired towards Americans. As you can guess highly technical software is need to have a missile take out another missile or aircraft at multiple times the speed of sound.

The software problem with the Patriot missile system is that it used several computers that needed to be in sync in order to properly detect and track targets. A software bug caused these computer to slight become out of sync if the system was operating for periods of time, without rebooting.

On February 25, 1991 in Dhahran, Saudi Arabia a Patriot missile system was ran continuously for over 100 hours. This only caused the systems to be off by a few tenths of a second, but when a Patriot missile flies at MACH 5 this is to a distance of hundreds of meters[10].

This software glitch caused the death of 28 Americans.
